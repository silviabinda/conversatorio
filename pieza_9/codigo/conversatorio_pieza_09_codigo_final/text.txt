Gender Inequality: In many societies, gender inequality persists, which can lead to the devaluation of women's lives and rights. This inequality can manifest in various forms, such as unequal access to education, employment, and economic resources. 
Cultural Norms: Cultural norms that perpetuate harmful gender stereotypes, normalize violence against women, or condone discriminatory practices can contribute to femicides. Economic Factors: Economic disparities and poverty can exacerbate violence against women. 
Economic dependence on an abusive partner can make it difficult for victims to leave dangerous situations.
